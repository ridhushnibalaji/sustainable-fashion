Fashion that Doesn't Cost Our Earth


Introduction

Every morning, we open our closets and pick out what to wear. Maybe you scroll online and see people post their GRWMs and OOTDs. But when was the last time you wondered where those clothes actually came from? The reality is that most of us don't. That shirt you threw on this morning and the jeans you've worn a hundred times are just part of your routine. While they may just be a small part of your life, the manufacturing process of textiles devastates our planet.

What is Sustainable Fashion?

Sustainable fashion is clothing that's designed, manufactured, and distributed in a way that is environmentally friendly and responsible. The rise of fast fashion has contributed to a growing waste crisis, as well as the depletion of natural resources because of its promotion of rapid production and disposable clothing. The movement towards sustainability is about creating a system for clothing that can work long-term without wasting resources or abusing people. While awareness of sustainability grows, balancing personal style, affordability, and environmental responsibility can be hard. Even so, there are actions you can take in your daily lives to support this cause.

Fast Fashion

  • Cheap, low quality
  • High waste production
  • Synthetic materials
  • Exploits labor

Sustainable Fashion

  • Higher quality, long lasting
  • Reduced waste
  • Natural/recycled fibers
  • Ethical labor practices


Explore Sustainable Choices


Thrifting

With old trends coming back into fashion, thrifting is a popular way to find clothes you want without buying newly made ones. Thrift shops sell clothing from donations, surplus from retailers and manufacturers, charity drives, and auctions. This is why you can find a variety of styles and brands in them, even rare designer items.

Donate or Recycle Old Clothes

Instead of throwing away clothes you no longer wear, consider donating or recycling them. Giving your clothes a second life not only helps reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills, but it also supports people in need. Worn-out fabrics can be turned into new materials, keeping them in use and reducing the demand for new resources.

Organic Materials & Sustainable Brands

Buying clothing made from brands that use organic materials, is a great way to be more sustainable. Look for certifications to make sure they are eco-friendly and pay fair wages to workers. Support brands that make sure each step of the process is safe.

DIY & Upcycling

A great alternative to buying new clothes is upcycling. You can revamp your old or boring clothes by sewing them differently, adding extra elements, or fixing the size/holes. Basic sewing skills can go a long way! This lets you make your closet more unique without spending much money. There are DIYs online that show tutorials on how to add prints to shirts, paint jeans, and so many more options. Upcycling not only reduces waste but also allows you to express your personal style in a sustainable way.

Media Literacy

Media literacy is all about understanding and questioning the messages we see in advertising and social media. Learning to recognize how brands influence trends and consumer choices helps you make more conscious decisions about what you buy. Ask yourself how often you'll wear something before you buy it. Try not to buy things off impulse or microtrends and pay attention to misleading ads.
